What is Blogging?

Blogging in the context of travel and tourism involves individuals or organizations creating and sharing content about their travel experiences and related information online. This content can take various forms, including written articles, photos, videos, and even virtual reality experiences, and is typically presented in a blog format. Travel blogs serve as a platform for sharing personal narratives, providing practical travel advice, and promoting destinations and travel-related businesses.

Writing a travel blog is not only a fun way to remember your travels. It can even be a money maker.
There are several ways you can monetize your blog to create a secondary, and maybe a primary, source of income. If you have decided to launch a travel blog, there are things you should do to ensure that your blog is designed for success – and attracts the attention of brands, sponsors, travel journalists, and PR companies.

Thanks For Looking!

I'm David Leiter, I'm an American who's been traveling the world full time for 9 years now.
I started this travel blog in 2019 to document my own international trips, share my photos, and help others learn how to travel the world and find some really good spots off the beaten path.

All of the writing and pictures on this site come from my own personal experience.
My second home is Bali, Indonesia, where I met my wife Intan, who’s a Bali local. Now she joins me on these adventures too.

Together, we've done some bucket list hikes, climbed active volcanoes, seen exotic wildlife, and visited some spectacular castles, temples, and monuments around the world. I've worked with and been featured by BBC Travel, NBC News, Time, and other companies.
I haven’t been everywhere, but it’s on my list. I hope this world travel blog can help and inspire you in your own journeys as well!

Sailing Zatara

After two decades of corporate world grind, we decided our family needed a change. In summer of 2016, we sold our Texas home and bought a sailboat. Oh, and I should mention: we’d never sailed before. Our plan seemed absolutely insane!

Fast forward 8 years and we’ve successfully circumnavigated the globe, sailed over 50,000 nautical miles, visited 50+ countries, said goodbye to one crew member, and gained and lost a cat. Our kids have basically been raised at sea, and are better humans because of it!

We film our adventures and share them every Thursday on YouTube. All our episodes are family-friendly and filmed, edited, and published exclusively by us! We would be honored to have you follow along on our journey!
